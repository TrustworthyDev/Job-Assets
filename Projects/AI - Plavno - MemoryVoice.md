MemoryVoice - AI Voice Assistant for Memory Care, Patient Communication & Senior Care Support

Plavno developed MemoryVoice, an AI-powered memory care and patient communication solution designed for senior care providers, assisted living operators, homecare teams, and digital health platforms.

#### Key Metrics & Impact
MemoryVoice helps people living with memory loss stay oriented, recall meaningful moments, and remain emotionally connected through calm, voice-based interaction. Built as a human-centered layer for memory care software, homecare software, and patient engagement workflows, the platform supports both patients and caregivers in daily care routines.
- Up to 25–35% fewer repetitive caregiver explanations after introducing contextual voice support
- Up to 20–30% better daily routine adherence in supported care scenarios
- Stronger emotional engagement compared to reminder-only interaction flows
- Lower frequency of confusion-driven anxiety episodes in day-to-day support use
- Better caregiver efficiency through reduced repetitive communication workload
- More consistent patient orientation and memory recall support over time

01. Problem
In assisted living, homecare, and memory care environments, patients with Alzheimer's and other memory-related conditions often struggle with basic orientation and routine continuity.

Questions such as:
"Did I eat today?", "Where am I?", "What happened earlier?"

can repeat many times a day.

For care teams and families, this creates both emotional strain and operational inefficiency:

- Repeated explanations throughout the day
- Increased anxiety and confusion episodes
- Fragmented communication between patients, caregivers, and families
- Limited visibility into orientation and recall patterns
- Too much reliance on human intervention for simple but emotionally sensitive support

Traditional reminder apps and care tools help with tasks, but they do not provide the kind of contextual, emotionally aware communication needed in memory care and senior care settings.

02. Challenge
The goal was to create more than a reminder tool. The platform needed to function as an intelligent support layer across memory care, patient communication, and caregiver workflows.

The system had to:
- Support patients with cognitive decline without overwhelming them
- Understand emotional context during voice interactions
- Reconstruct relevant memories from fragmented personal data
- Improve patient communication in a calm, human-centered way
- Fit into senior care, assisted living, or homecare software environments
- Maintain privacy and healthcare-grade data protection

There were also important implementation constraints:
- Personal context such as photos, voice notes, family history, and routines had to be transformed into structured, retrievable memory support
- The platform needed to work for both patients and caregivers simultaneously
- Voice interaction had to remain simple and reassuring in real-world care settings
- The system had to support long-term daily use rather than short one-time sessions

#### AI Companion for Memory Care & Connection
MemoryVoice is a voice AI companion that helps patients make sense of the present, recall meaningful moments, follow routines, and reconnect with loved ones. A unique feature lets families create a digital voice identity, so the assistant speaks with the voice of someone familiar — making interactions more personal and comforting.

## User Flows
- Ask: A patient asks a question about their day, routine, family member, location, or a past event.
- Interpret: The assistant analyzes emotional tone, conversational context, and memory signals to understand the type of support needed.
- Retrieve: The system retrieves relevant routine details, personal memories, orientation cues, or family context from structured memory data.
- Respond: The patient receives a calm and empathetic answer designed to improve orientation, reduce confusion, and support emotional safety.
- Support Care Teams: Caregivers and family members can update context, review interaction patterns, and reduce repetitive communication burdens across daily care.

#### Deep Dive: Project Architecture
- Voice Interaction Layer: Voice-first experience designed for calm, low-friction communication with patients experiencing memory decline.
- Conversational AI Layer: Natural language understanding and response orchestration for emotionally aware patient communication.
- Memory Support Layer: Structured memory architecture that organizes routines, family context, personal history, photos, audio, and notes into meaningful retrieval logic.
- Behavioral Intelligence Layer: Tracks interaction patterns, routine adherence, confusion triggers, and support needs over time.
- Caregiver & Family Layer: Workflows for updating personal context, reviewing interaction patterns, and improving care continuity.
